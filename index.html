Fernando Letelier

Ph.D. Candidate in Economics · Rutgers University

Welcome! I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at Rutgers University. My research lies at the intersection of international macroeconomics and international trade, with a unifying focus on how different forms of heterogeneity alter shock transmission, aggregate dynamics, and policy implications in open and emerging economies.

My dissertation spans three interconnected areas: firm productivity heterogeneity and its implications for international transportation costs and trade outcomes; heterogeneity in inflation shocks and how different agents form inflation expectations; and cross-country heterogeneity in production structures — commodity exporters versus non-commodity exporters — and the resulting differences in business cycle dynamics and sudden stop severity.

I am on the economics job market in 2025–2026 and will be available for interviews at the ASSA Annual Meetings in January 2026.

Research

Work in Progress

Heterogeneous Expectations Across Inflation Regimes: Evidence and Implications for Monetary Policy
With Francisco Luna

We study how the ability of different agents to anticipate inflation in the United States varies with the nature of the inflationary shock and the macroeconomic context. We compare the behavior of professional forecasters and households to understand when expectations become more persistent and backward-looking versus when they incorporate forward-looking information more efficiently, with implications for inflation persistence and the transmission of monetary policy.
